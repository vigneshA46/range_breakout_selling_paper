RANGE BREAKOUT STRATERGY 

This strategy begins by observing the NIFTY index on the 5-minute chart and marking the open and close of the 9:55–10:00 candle, where the higher of the open/close becomes the top line and the lower becomes the bottom line. The close of that candle is used as the ATM reference, from which option strikes are selected: CE = ATM − 400 and PE = ATM + 400. After strike selection, the index chart is switched to 1-minute resolution while keeping the same marked levels, and both the selected CE and PE are traded using 1-minute option candles. From 10:01 onward, trades are evaluated independently using the index: if any 1-minute index candle closes above the top line, and the candle’s average price ((O+H+L+C)/4) is above the top line and below the candle close, a PE sell signal is generated, and the actual PE entry is executed on the next candle at market price. The PE position is exited when any index candle closes back below the top line. Similarly, if any candle closes below the bottom line, and the candle’s average price is below the bottom line and above the candle close, a CE sell signal is generated, and the CE entry is executed on the next candle. The CE position is exited when any index candle closes back above the bottom line. Each option position starts with a fixed SL of −15 points and a trailing stop of −30 points from entry, but the SL and TSL become active only after price first moves beyond the TSL level, and as price continues in favor, both the SL and TSL trail forward in steps of 10 points. In addition, a day-level risk rule is enforced: and if total MTM reaches −₹2500, all positions are closed and trading stops for the day.
